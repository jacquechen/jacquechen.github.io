This is an documentation of some of my student projects created in relation to my architecture and landscape architecture interests. Feel free to click around the site and explore some of my other interests.

Free Little Library

The greenspace across the street from MIT's Mass Ave entrance was the site of one of the Institute's former dorms, Bexley Hall. Since the demolition of Bexley Hall, the newly constructed park lies largely underutilized. The production of a set of tripod libraries become hosts to a variety of literature types dispersed around the green space. the strong character of their form extends as an invitation to others to explore an otherwise static place.

Fragmented Impressions

The purpose of these tiles is to create high fidelity records of native plants collected in the local landscape. Separating the plants into its leaves, root systems, stems, flowers, etc. can allow us to rearrange and create abstracted impressions of our natural surroundings.

The inital idea for the tiles come from the understanding that in historically rich regions like the West Bank, the land often becomes closely linked to one's identity and cultural narrative.
